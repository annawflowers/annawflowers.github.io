Anna Williamson Flowers

designer, patternmaker, and sewist
based in Brooklyn, NY

I am a Brooklyn based designer and seamstress. My work is designed, patterned, cut, and sewn in the comforts of my studio (sometimes I brainstorm on the 4 train).

I've always loved to sew, because of the unique combination of art and math that's involved in the craft. As a child of engineers, I was raised on math. It's not so clear where the art came from. Designing is creative and imaginitive. Pattern-making is a geometry puzzle. Stitching is a technically demanding craft.

Before I pick up a needle and thread, I start with a 2-D model of my subject. I take dozens of measurements and combine them to create a baseline pattern for the subject's body. This pattern is called a sloper, and it becomes the basis for a well fitted design. Slopers also make great wall art if you're in the market for a non-traditional self-portrait.

The most important elements that come into my designs are structure, silhouette, and function. I favor classically feminine silhouettes - accentuated waistlines, fitted tops, full skirts. After that the fun is in the details. I believe strongly that if it can have pockets, it should have pockets.

I have a lot of thoughts about the perceptions we hold about fashion and sewing. The words we use to describe people who are interested in these things are particularly telling. Think about the connotations of "fashionista" and "seamstress" for a start. I plan to write something in detail about this, and when I do it will be published here.






Commision Work

If you're interested in commissioning a piece from me, send an email to annawflowers@gmail.com. Please include as much detail as you can about what you are looking for. If you've been dreaming about something forever and have an inspiration board on Pinterest, include a link! If all you know is that you want a new dress for wedding season, that's fine too. Please also include the date when you would like to wear the piece.

To make a custom piece, I'll start by taking your measurements and creating a basic pattern called a sloper, to fit your body. We'll check the fit of the sloper and make any adjustments necessary before I start on the garment itself. When the garment is finished, we'll do another fitting to make sure everything is exactly how you want it. If not, I'll make adjustmets until you're happy.

Pricing will depend on the complexity of the piece and the materials required.
